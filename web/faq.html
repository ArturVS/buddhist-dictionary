Frequently Asked Questions

  1. Question: What is the Chinese-Sanskrit-English Buddhist Dictionary?

    Answer: See the About page.

  2. Question: Who are the target users for the dictionary?

    Answer: See the Goals here.

  3. Question: How do I use the dictionary and language tools?

    Answer: See the page Translation and Document Analysis Workflows.

  4. Question: Is this a glossary?

    Answer: No. The dictionary is not an encyclopedia or a glossary of Buddhist terms. Please see the References page for some excellent source of English and Chinese explanations of Buddhist terms.

  5. Question: Is this a Chinese-English dictionary?

    Answer: Yes. The project started out as a generic Chinese dictionary and works OK for that. Most of the terms in the dictionary are actually not Buddhist terms. I hope that people that used the dictionary before I specialized it to Buddhism keep using it. A full Chinese dictionary is retained to assist in analyzing blocks of Chinese text without the reader having to switch between a regular Chinese dictionary and a Buddhist Chinese dictionary.

  6. Question: Can I copy stuff out of the dictionary?

    Answer: Yes, if you attribute the source appropriately. You should attribute the source of the data to the NTI Chinese-Sanskrit-English Buddhist Dictionary under the terms of the Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 3.0 License license.

  7. Question: How can I add words to the dictionary or join the project?

    Answer: Please do. To join the project send an email with your GitHub id and a note introducting yourself to alex@chinesenotes.com.

  8. Question: Can I just add one word to the dictionary without actually joining the project?

    Answer: Please create a GitHub issue describing the word to add. Please include the Chinese and English text of the word, the grammar or type of word you are adding and any other notes about it. Names of places, people, and institutions are fine too. Here is an example of a ticket to add an entry to the dictionary: Example ticket to add a dictionary entry. You can also create tickets to add a character, synonym, or to correct an entry.

  9. Question: What is a dictionary entry?

    Answer: Primary dictionary entries can be a word, a freqently occurring phrase, or the name person, place, or organization, with a mapping to another languages. The mappings supported today are Chinese to English and Sanskrit to Chinese with English. Because of the search capabilities the dictionary can be searched in reverse order and for complete phrases as well.

  10. Question: How big is the dictionary?

    Answer: As of December 2013 the dictionary has 32 889 Chinese-English word entries, 3 443 Character entries, 2 900 Buddhist word entries, 1 145 Sanskrit-Chinese word entries, plus synonyms and related words.
